Introduction to Katakana

Katakana is one of the Japanese writing systems. It's a phonetic script primarily used for foreign words, loan words, technical and scientific terms, and for emphasis. Each character represents a syllable.

Katakana characters are more angular and straightforward than hiragana, making them easier to distinguish in some cases.

Tips for Learning Katakana

  • Focus on learning katakana after you're comfortable with hiragana.
  • Many katakana characters resemble their hiragana counterparts or have similar stroke patterns.
  • Practice by trying to read foreign brand names and words that are commonly written in katakana.
  • Use mnemonics to remember the more challenging shapes.
  • The extended katakana characters are used to represent sounds that don't exist in traditional Japanese.
